What is Poverty?

Poverty is about not having enough money to meet basic needs including food, clothing and shelter. However, poverty is much more than just not having enough money. Poverty is lack of shelter.Poverty is the condition in which individuals or groups lack the financial resources and access to basic necessities needed for a decent standard of living. These necessities often include food, clean water, shelter, healthcare, education, and clothing. Poverty can manifest in various forms, ranging from absolute poverty (where people lack the basic necessities to survive) to relative poverty (where people may have enough for survival but still struggle compared to others in their society).

Solutions to End Poverty

    There are many ways to end poverty such as:

  • Education: Improving access to education to break the cycle of poverty.
  • Healthcare: Providing affordable healthcare to ensure healthy communities.
  • Economic Growth: Promoting sustainable economic development to create job opportunities.
  • Social Safety Nets: Ensuring that vulnerable populations are supported with welfare programs.
